IMPACT OF TWITTER

The social media platform, Twitter, has had an immense impact on many facets of our lives. Scroll down to learn more.

  • First, let me give you a brief overview of the platform's history.

  • Twitter was founded on March 21st, 2006 and launched in July 2006. It was first named "twttr" then changed to "Twitter" a few months later.

  • The platform began to grow in 2007 and became more and more popular each year.

  • It had 400,000 tweets posted per quarter in 2007,

  • 100 million tweets posted per quarter in 2008,

  • 50 million tweets per day by February 2010,

  • about 65 million tweets per day by June 2010,

  • 140 million tweets per day by March 2011,

  • 340 million tweets per day by March 2012,

  • and over 400 million tweets per day by September 2013.

  • Nowadays, it has about 500 million tweets per day.

  • Yeah, that's a lot of tweets.

  • Fun fact: "Twitter” not only means “chirps from birds,” but it also means “a short burst of inconsequential information"

  • So like...what has Twitter impacted?

  • Twitter has impacted A LOT of things. To name a few, Twitter has impacted...

  • politics,

  • news,

  • and ~society~.

  • So, let's get into it
    (and by that I mean scroll down).

  • Let's go in order and start with politics.

  • Politics? Yikes, that's a hot topic.

  • Yeah, but it's important to talk about how much politics has been impacted by one social media platform.

  • Twitter's political era largely began during the Obama administration, when the @POTUS account was created.

  • Nowadays, EVERY Senator and House Representitive has a Twitter account. Basically, if you're trying to get elected, you probably have a Twitter account.

  • Officials use these accounts to quickly make a statement directly to the people rather than having to make an official statement.

  • Also, these Twitter statements can reach A LOT of people.

  • First, lets briefly talk about the Trump administration.

  • During his 2016 and 2020 campaigns, Trump did what few politicians had done before: use his Twitter account every day to share his raw opinions.

  • Nowadays, this type of candor is used by more and more officials. Though, maybe not to the same extent.

  • Trump often used Twitter to insult people, including world leaders, which sometimes caused quite a stir around the globe.

  • Besides Trump, Twitter has increasingly been used by citizens of all political ideologies, which is kind of a double-edged sword.

  • On one hand, this allows people to easily connect with others who share the same ideology.

  • On the other hand, it is easy to ONLY connect with others who share the same ideology, meaning people aren't getting exposed to any differing ideas.

  • This can cause people to become extremely biased and polarized, and enter a "groupthink" mentality.

  • Alright, enough about politics. Now let's talk about the news.

  • Twitter has affected both how news is shared and how news is consumed.

  • For starters, every news station and newspaper is on Twitter.

  • These stations and papers tweet news details and news articles, effectively negating the need to watch the news or read a newspaper.

  • It is also a way for people to get news updates ASAP, rather than having to wait for an article to be updated or a newscaster to say it.

  • Twitter itself has somewhat become a news hub - its Twitter Moments wrap up tweets about trending news stories and share them within Twitter's search tab.

  • In the United States, 71% of Twitter users use the platform to read news.

  • It is safe to say that news has become a major part of this social media platform.

  • Can we talk about something a little more interesting now?

  • Yes! Let's talk about ~society~

  • Specifically, activism.

  • Twitter has had a major role in many movements. It has given a voice everyday people, allowing movements to grow FAST.

  • In fact, one retweet can expose a tweet to nearly 500,000 people.

  • Today, lets talk about #BlackLivesMatter.

  • #BlackLivesMatter first appeared on Twitter in July 2013, and at first it wasn't used much.

  • But then, in November 2014, its usage spiked when Tamir Rice was killed by police while he was playing with a toy gun.

  • On this day, the hashtag was used nearly 175,000 times. From then on, this hashtag has been used often whenever racial injustice occurs.

  • The hashtag has created a major movement,

  • notably in 2020. In this year, from May 22 to August 22, there were more than 10,600 BLM protests in the United States,

  • mostly because of Twitter.

  • So, Twitter can have an amazing effect on activism. But, it can also cause some issues.

  • Twitter, and other social media platforms, have created this "hashtag activism" which is seem by some people as a form of "slacktivism".

  • Basically, hashtags can spread awareness about a movement,

  • but they can also give people the illusion that using the hashtag is fully effective in creating change and no other action needs to be done.

  • When, in reality, change mainly happens via protests, calling legislators, etc.

  • So, hashtag activism is seen by some people as fake activism.

  • Though, I think we can all agree that hashtag activism can bring major awareness to an issue, which might then lead to actual change.
